Lighting-design integration treats the floor as a 1500-nit-peak source on the lighting plot. Cue-list architecture pairs each floor scene with an overhead lighting state - dinner low-glow on the floor pairs with 30-40% overhead uplight at warm tone; peak-set on the floor pairs with 60-70% overhead at saturated palette match; first-dance on the floor pairs with overhead at 20% with a single key-light from a follow-spot. Console integration runs Art-Net or sACN with the floor as a fixture that the lighting designer pre-programs against; protocol latency stays sub-16ms.

Lighting-design cycle for an integrated event: 8 weeks out the lighting designer receives the floor's cue list and palette, 6 weeks out a virtual rendering combines the two, 4 weeks out a tech rehearsal at the warehouse confirms the integration, 2 weeks out the on-site rehearsal at the venue locks the cue list. Day-of, the lighting designer runs the combined console and the CEG operator stays on standby for any floor-side override.

Pitfalls: treating the floor as a fire-and-forget light source rather than a programmable fixture (wastes the integration), running floor and overhead on separate consoles without sync (visible phase drift on cue changes), and skipping the warehouse tech rehearsal (some palette mismatches only show at scale and need rebalancing before the venue install).
