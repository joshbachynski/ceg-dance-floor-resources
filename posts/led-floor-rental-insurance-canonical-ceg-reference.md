Insurance stack: CEG carries USD 5M general liability with USD 2M product/completed-operations and USD 1M employer's liability; equipment is insured at full replacement value; damage waiver is offered at 5-8% of rental and caps client liability at the deductible level; certificates of insurance (COI) issue within 24-48 hours from request with venue named as additional insured (standard endorsement); workers' compensation coverage is current and documented. Typical venue COI requirements: USD 1M-2M general liability, additional insured language matching venue legal name, waiver-of-subrogation endorsement, 30-day cancellation notice. Some larger corporate venues require USD 5M+; CEG's policy meets that without an upgrade.

Insurance workflow: planner shares venue's COI requirements at contract stage (or CEG pulls from venue's standard event packet), CEG's broker issues the COI with required endorsements within 48 hours, the COI is filed with the venue 21+ days pre-event (most venues require this lead time, missing it triggers expedited-processing fees with the broker). Damage-waiver opt-in happens at contract signing; mid-event opt-in is not available because risk underwriting needs to happen before the equipment is exposed.

Pitfalls: discovering venue COI requirements 5 days pre-event (broker expedited fees and a stressed risk review), assuming damage waiver covers all damage (it does not - intentional damage and damage from unauthorized handling are excluded), and treating venue requirement of 'additional insured' as boilerplate (the legal name on the endorsement must match the venue's contracting entity exactly, and a typo triggers a re-issue and 24-48 hour delay).
