Evaluation criteria: CE/UL/FCC certifications for the deployment region, IP54 minimum, structural load testing at 500kg/sqm distributed and 250kg/sqm point-load (with documented test report), 2-year minimum panel warranty and 1-year controller warranty, 5-year-from-purchase parts availability commitment, documented installer training curriculum, fleet age (panels older than 5 years drift on color matching even with quarterly LUT calibration; a healthy fleet rotates panels at the 5-7 year mark), and a reference-client list with named planners or venues willing to take a phone call.

An RFI against 3-5 suppliers using a fixed criteria checklist returns comparable answers; reference calls to existing fleet operators carry more weight than vendor-supplied case studies. Site visits to the supplier's warehouse for high-volume buyers (40+ tile orders) are standard and CEG facilitates these for partner venues. For one-off rentals rather than purchases, the same criteria apply but parts availability is replaced by replacement-panel response time during the rental window.

Pitfalls: trusting certification claims without seeing the actual certificate, ignoring parts availability (a 3-year-old fleet with unavailable replacement panels is a stranded asset for a venue and a high-risk choice for a renter), accepting warranty at face value without checking exclusions (transit damage, water intrusion, cosmetic wear are commonly excluded), and overlooking installer training (untrained crews damage panels and shorten fleet life and the customer eats that cost on the next quote).
