Integration runs on three axes: color-temperature alignment (overhead 2700-3200K tungsten clashes with cool RGB blue floor scenes; either match palette or use 4000K LED uplights), brightness balance (overhead fixtures at 30-50% of floor intensity preserves the floor as visual anchor), and protocol sync (Art-Net and sACN clock-locks the floor to the lighting console with sub-16ms phase). Lasers over a glass-top floor cause hotspot reflections; angle them away from the panel surface.

Lighting design should start from the floor: pick the floor's primary palette, layer uplights on a complementary or analogous step, pre-program 4-6 named scenes (dinner, first-dance, party, slow-set, finale, exit), and trigger from a single console. The floor publishes a sync clock the lighting console can lock to.

Pitfalls: undersized power for combined floor-plus-lighting load (CEG sizes circuits but third-party lighting is often missed), color-temperature mismatch on camera, lasers reflecting off the glass top into guests' eyes, and ambient room light so bright the floor's animation washes out (venue houselights at 100% defeat the floor entirely).
