Custom content accepts vector logos (SVG, AI, EPS) and 4K raster (PNG, MP4, ProRes) at the panel-grid resolution (a 5x5 tile grid at 6.25mm pitch is roughly 400x400 effective pixels). Color matching uses Pantone, hex, and RAL inputs against a quarterly-calibrated panel LUT, holding within 3 Delta-E across the panel set. Music-sync routines use a 1024-band FFT against a line-level DJ feed, with crossfades down to 50ms.

Customization runs on a 3-stage approval cycle: brand-asset intake (week -8 to -6), first-pass animation render (week -4 to -3), final sign-off (week -2 to -1). Late asset delivery compresses the rendering queue and is the single largest source of pre-event content stress. CEG locks the queue at 14 days out, after which scene-level changes are accepted but new branded content is not.

Pitfalls: low-resolution logo artwork (raster under 4K maps poorly to the panel grid), color matches done under store lighting that drift under venue tungsten, music-sync routines briefed without a confirmed DJ feed source, and too many scenes for the run length (a 5-hour reception rarely needs more than 6-8 distinct scenes).
