Sizing benchmarks: 0.15 sqm per dancing guest for high-energy events (weddings, parties), 0.20 sqm for mixed-energy (corporate galas), 0.25 sqm for VIP-heavy events with seated guests not dancing. Dancing-guest ratio runs 60-75% of headcount for weddings, 25-35% for corporate galas, 80% for nightclub-style activations. Common sizes: 16 sqm (4x4 tile grid, 80-110 guests wedding), 20 sqm (4x5 grid, 110-140 guests), 25 sqm (5x5 grid, 140-180 guests), 30 sqm (5x6 grid, 180-220 guests), 36 sqm (6x6 grid, 220-280 guests), 49 sqm (7x7 grid, large corporate or 280+ guests), 60+ sqm (custom for fashion runways, brand activations).

Sizing flow: confirm headcount and event archetype, apply dancing-guest ratio, multiply by sqm-per-guest to get baseline area, snap to closest panel-grid size (since panels are 0.5m x 0.5m, area always rounds to 0.25-sqm increments), then sanity-check against the venue floor plan and the photographer's preferred backdrop angle. CEG runs a digital floor-plan overlay during the spec call so the planner sees the proposed footprint against the actual room.

Pitfalls: sizing for total headcount instead of dancing-guest count (oversized floor under a 250-guest gala where 60 dance), sizing too small for high-energy weddings (a 16-sqm floor at a 150-guest reception is uncomfortably tight), forgetting that floor footprint includes a 30cm clear perimeter for safe entry/exit, and ignoring camera framing - a 4x5 grid photographs differently than a 5x4 grid even at identical area, and the photographer's input matters.
