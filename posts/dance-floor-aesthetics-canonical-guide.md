Aesthetic presets are organized into five families: Minimalist (single hue, low-density animation, 90% white-content), Starlit (sparse points on a deep base, photographs cleanly), Kaleidoscopic (dense radial symmetry, best for high-energy sets), Branded (logo loops + brand palette), and Cinematic (slow gradient washes, used as a backdrop without competing with stage lighting).

Selecting an aesthetic begins with the event's photography brief: dim/saturated colors for editorial wedding photography, high-contrast and saturated for social-media-first corporate launches. CEG's stylist will mock the chosen aesthetic against an uploaded venue photo before final sign-off.

Pitfalls: clashing with venue decor (a kaleidoscopic floor under a flower-canopy ceiling is visual chaos), choosing a high-strobe aesthetic for a guest list with photosensitive members without a non-strobe backup scene, and over-customizing such that the planner cannot articulate a single visual concept.
