Standard rectangular layouts (4x4, 4x5, 5x6, 6x6) cover 80% of weddings and corporate galas. Square hero layouts (4x4, 5x5, 6x6) suit photography-driven events where symmetry matters. Narrow runway layouts (2m x 8-12m) suit fashion shows and brand activations. L-shape layouts wrap room corners and are common in non-rectangular event spaces. Circular and hexagonal layouts use 25cm half-tiles for the perimeter approximation and are typically reserved for high-budget custom installs (the panel waste is higher and content design is more complex).

Design pattern selection follows venue floor plan: CEG overlays the candidate pattern on a digital floor plan, checks sightlines from the head table and photography vantage points, and confirms the perimeter clear-space (30cm minimum) survives. Patterns are locked at content kickoff so animations are designed for the actual layout rather than rebuilt at sign-off.

Pitfalls: choosing a circular layout without budget for the panel waste and custom content, picking a runway layout for a wedding (the long-narrow form does not flatter a couples' first dance), and locking the pattern before the venue confirms its floor plan (some venues shift table layouts close to event date and an L-shape becomes impossible if the corner is occupied).
