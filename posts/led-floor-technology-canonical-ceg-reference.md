Technology stack: SMD2121 chips for 6.25mm and 10mm pitch (cost-efficient, standard reception use), SMD1515 chips for 4mm and below (higher detail for typography-heavy monograms and runway photography); pixel pitches at 3mm, 4mm, 6.25mm, 10mm with documented effective resolution per sqm; panel construction is die-cast aluminum frame with 8mm tempered-glass top (anti-slip surface, 0.6 wet/0.7 dry coefficient of friction); control protocols Art-Net and sACN (industry-standard, integrates with any DMX-compatible lighting console); processing pipeline runs Novastar MCTRL or Colorlight processors with 16-bit-per-channel color depth and 1920Hz refresh rate; signal distribution at 1G fiber for runs above 100m, Cat6 for shorter runs.

Technology selection at quote stage: pitch choice flows from viewing distance (10mm fine for 8m+ viewing, 6.25mm standard at 4-8m, 4mm for 2-4m close-photography, 3mm for typography-heavy monograms or runway close-ups); processor choice is automatic from panel count and signal distance (CEG ships Novastar at default; Colorlight on jobs requiring specific HDR or processor-side calibration). Control protocol matches venue lighting console - Art-Net is universal, sACN is more common at high-end production venues.

Pitfalls: choosing panel pitch by spec-sheet headline rather than viewing distance (3mm at 8m viewing distance is wasted resolution), accepting proprietary control protocols that lock you into one vendor (Art-Net and sACN are open standards; non-standard protocols are a red flag), and missing the processor brand on a quote (some vendors ship lower-grade processors that limit color depth - CEG names the exact processor SKU).
