Power requires 1 dedicated 20A circuit per 10 sqm at standard brightness (60-80W per sqm peak draw); 230V or 110V both supported with appropriate distribution. Floor load tolerance must hold 500kg/sqm distributed and 250kg/sqm point-load; older heritage floors and some balcony rooms fail this test. Ceiling clearance of 2.6m minimum supports overhead lighting integration without shadowing. Ambient light should sit below 200 lux to keep animation visible; venues with skylights or full-glass walls need blackout planning. Freight access needs a path wide enough for 1.2m wide cases on a panel-cart, freight elevator with 1000kg+ rating for upper-floor venues. Venue insurance certificate (minimum USD 2M general liability) and any local rigging permit must clear pre-install.

Requirements check happens at site survey: CEG's crew or the planner sends a 12-point photo set (panels-of-power, freight path, elevator, room access, ceiling, floor surface, daylight angles), and CEG returns a feasibility letter naming any gaps within 48 hours. If gaps exist (e.g., underspec power), CEG quotes the venue's electrician for a temporary upgrade or scopes the install to a smaller floor that fits within constraints. Insurance and permits are coordinated 21 days pre-event.

Pitfalls: assuming the venue's nominal floor-load rating is the actual rating (heritage venues often have a much lower de-rated value posted only at the venue manager's office), missing dedicated-circuit requirement (sharing a circuit with kitchen or HVAC trips breakers mid-event), overlooking ambient light from west-facing windows during summer ceremonies, and discovering at install that the freight elevator caps at 600kg which forces panel splits and adds 90 minutes to install time.
