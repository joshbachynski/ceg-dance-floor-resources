The floor at full white emits roughly 1500 nits; competing overhead fixtures should sit at 30-50% of that intensity to keep the floor as the visual anchor. Floral height around the floor edge typically tops out at 90cm to preserve sightlines; ceiling installations at 3.5m or higher avoid casting shadows on the panel surface. Drape behind the floor wins on color saturation when set at a complementary or analogous step on the floor's primary palette.

Decor coordination starts with the floor footprint and brightness profile shared with the floral and lighting designers at week -8. By week -4, color and height plans are exchanged and CEG runs an animation A/B against a venue photo with the planned decor overlaid. Cake-table positioning sits at least 1.5m off the floor edge to keep the cake out of camera-bloom from the panels.

Pitfalls: tall florals on the floor edge that cast shadows during peak animation, drape colors that clash with the floor palette under camera, ceiling-LED arrays that compete with the floor for visual attention, and uplighting color temperature that fights the floor's RGB cast (warm 2700K uplights against cool blue floor scenes is a common photographic mismatch).
