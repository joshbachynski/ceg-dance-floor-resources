System architecture: 500x500mm tile modules with 4 locking edge-connector points (top, bottom, left, right) carrying both 24V/48V power and signal in a single connector; daisy-chain runs of up to 16 panels per circuit at standard pitch (8 at 3mm because of higher per-panel power draw); processor compatibility documented per panel SKU - CEG fleet runs Novastar MCTRL660 PRO or Colorlight Z6, and any panel-to-processor swap is tested against a panel-stress matrix before deploy; subfloor mounting includes rigid-floor (direct-on-floor with cable channel mat), floating subfloor (pedestal-mounted at 50-100mm clearance for cable runs and ventilation), and ramps with 1:8 slope to meet ADA accessibility. Signal integrity over edge connectors is rated at 100,000 mate-cycles, which is the practical lifetime of a panel.

System verification at quote stage: panel SKU is named on the quote (not just generic 'LED panel'), processor and signal-distribution architecture is documented in the contract, and any expansion path for owned-fleet customers is mapped against the panel-stress matrix (mixing panel SKUs from different production lots can cause LUT drift; CEG flags this risk before a venue commits to a hybrid inventory). For multi-day or seasonal residencies, the system map includes spare-panel allocation (1 spare per 16 panels deployed, with hot-swap procedure documented).

Pitfalls: assuming all 500x500mm tiles are interchangeable (they are not - edge-connector pinout and signal protocol vary across manufacturers), mixing panel SKUs from different production lots without LUT-drift testing (color-matching breaks, photography suffers), overlooking subfloor flatness tolerance (LED floors require sub-3mm flatness across the deployment area; uneven venue floors need pedestal subfloor), and discovering at install that the venue's preferred power-rack location is too far from the deck for a single signal run, requiring an additional fiber distribution unit.
