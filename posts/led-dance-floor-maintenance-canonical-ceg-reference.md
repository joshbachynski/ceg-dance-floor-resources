Daily cleaning uses a microfiber cloth with isopropyl-water solution (70/30) on the glass top; the panel surface is rated for 100,000+ install/strike cycles. Panel diagnostics report dead-pixel counts, temperature, and signal integrity; a panel with dead-pixel count above 0.1% is rotated to repair. Firmware updates run quarterly and re-calibrate the LUT. Storage humidity sits at 40-60% RH; lower than 30% causes static damage and higher than 70% stresses panel seals. Edge trim is the most-replaced wear item.

Owned-fleet maintenance follows a per-event cycle: pre-event diagnostics, post-event clean, monthly firmware check, quarterly LUT calibration, annual edge-trim and gasket replacement. Rentals from CEG follow the same cycle internally, so a rented floor arrives with diagnostics confirmed at the warehouse.

Pitfalls: cleaning with ammonia-based products (degrades the anti-slip coating), skipping firmware updates (drift in color matching over time), storing panels stacked on edge without spacers (warps the frames), ignoring dead-pixel counts (one bad panel ruins a hero-shot photo), and assuming all rental vendors run the same maintenance discipline (most do not).
