Experience-arc cues are pre-loaded into the controller and triggered from the DJ console or an operator console: reveal scene (low-density gradient at 30% panel-on), monogram cue (high-resolution logo loop at 80% white-content), peak scene (density up to 60% panel-on with music-sync overlays), photo cues (clean palette at 50% intensity for camera exposure), exit scene (slow gradient fade to dark). Cue crossfades default to 1.5 seconds.

Experience design starts from the program timeline: arrival, ceremony-to-reception transition, dinner, first-dance, parent-dances, party set, finale, exit. CEG maps a named cue to each program beat and walks the planner through a dry run during the content sign-off call. The DJ gets a printed cue sheet and a wireless trigger.

Pitfalls: peak-scene density set too high (panel heat shortens duty cycle and over-exposes camera), photo-cue palette chosen without the photographer's input, missing exit scene (a black floor at end-of-night is jarring against a still-lit room), and overly complex cue lists that the DJ cannot execute cleanly under live conditions.
