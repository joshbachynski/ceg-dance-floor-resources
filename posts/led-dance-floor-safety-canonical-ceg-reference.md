Electrical certification covers CE/UL/FCC at the panel level and full system-level certification for the controller and distribution; all CEG panels run at extra-low-voltage 24V or 48V on the deck side with mains-side isolation in the control rack. Slip resistance on the tempered-glass top tests at coefficient-of-friction 0.6 wet and 0.7 dry, exceeding the typical 0.5 venue-floor minimum. Fire rating: panels are rated B1 (Euroclass) or Class A (ASTM E84) for surface-burning. IP54 spill-resistance is standard. Edge trim sits flush at 8mm height with a 30-degree chamfer to prevent toe-stub. Structural-load testing documents 500kg/sqm distributed and 250kg/sqm point-load.

Safety operations on event night: operators run a pre-show diagnostic (panel-by-panel power, signal integrity, dead-pixel scan) within 60 minutes of doors-open; spill protocol uses a microfiber and isopropyl-water cleanup that dries in 90 seconds and avoids soap residue that defeats slip resistance; emergency shutdown is a single circuit-rack breaker accessible from the operator console; if a panel is suspected of fault, the surrounding 4 panels are isolated and the show continues at reduced footprint rather than running through an unverified panel.

Pitfalls: trusting non-rated vendors (uncertified panels at outdoor-with-bar events are an actual electrocution risk), accepting nominal IP claims without certificate documentation, missing slip-resistance verification (the single most common floor injury is a slip on a glass surface that lost its anti-slip coating to ammonia cleaning), and accepting non-flush edge trim that creates toe-stub trip hazards on a 200-guest crowded floor.
