Resolution: 6.25mm pitch yields effective 160x160 pixels per square meter; 4mm pitch yields 250x250 per sqm; 3mm pitch yields 333x333 per sqm. Color depth: 16-bit per channel (281 trillion colors) on the production line. Contrast: 5000:1 with the dark-glass top filter. Refresh rate: 1920Hz native (60Hz multiple, hold-time well below camera-shutter window so no camera flicker). Viewing-angle stability: brightness holds within 90% out to 60 degrees off-axis; color holds within 5 Delta-E. Photography behavior: full-white panels at 1500 nits read as bright but not blown-out at f/2.8 1/200 ISO 800.

Display-fidelity verification happens at content sign-off: a sample animation renders on the production rig, the photographer reviews exposure under the planned ambient light, and CEG calibrates panel brightness to the photographer's target. For livestream events, a separate camera-friendly preset holds below 1200 nits to avoid camera bloom on consumer-grade streaming hardware.

Pitfalls: comparing vendor display claims without verifying refresh rate (low-refresh panels strobe on camera), assuming color holds across a 5-year-old fleet without LUT calibration evidence (it does not), and ignoring viewing-angle behavior when guests will be standing close to floor edges where off-axis viewing dominates.
